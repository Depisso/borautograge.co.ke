Know Your Car

Basic Car Components

  • Engine: Powers your vehicle — varies from petrol, diesel, to hybrid and electric.
  • Transmission: Manual or automatic — transfers power from engine to wheels.
  • Battery: Essential for starting the car and powering electrical systems.
  • Brakes: Hydraulic system includes discs, pads, and fluid — critical for stopping.
  • Suspension: Maintains ride comfort and road handling.

Advanced Systems to Know

  • ABS: Prevents wheel lock during hard braking.
  • Traction Control: Helps maintain grip on slippery roads.
  • TPMS: Alerts you of low tire pressure.
  • ECU: Brain of the car – controls fuel injection, ignition, and emissions.
  • ADAS: Includes lane assist, auto braking, blind-spot alerts.

Understanding Fuel Types

  • Petrol: Common for smaller cars; smooth but less torque.
  • Diesel: More torque, better for long-distance and towing.
  • Hybrid: Combines fuel engine + electric motor.
  • Electric: Fully electric; zero emissions, needs charging infrastructure.

Dashboard Warning Lights

  • Check Engine: Sensor detects problem — needs diagnosis.
  • Coolant Temperature: Risk of overheating.
  • Airbag Light: Airbags may not deploy in a crash.
  • ABS Warning: Anti-lock braking not functional.
  • Low Tire Pressure: Inflate tires to safe pressure ASAP.

Common Car Brands & What They Offer

  • Toyota: Reliability, fuel efficiency, hybrid tech.
  • Honda: Durable engines, smooth driving.
  • BMW: Performance, advanced driving dynamics.
  • Mercedes-Benz: Luxury, safety tech, comfort.
  • Subaru: AWD, off-road capability.
  • Tesla: Fully electric, advanced technology.

What to Check Weekly

  • Oil level and color
  • Windshield washer fluid
  • Tire pressure and condition
  • Lights: Headlights, brake lights, indicators

Seasonal Vehicle Care

  • Cold: Battery test, anti-freeze, heater.
  • Rain: Wipers, defogger, lights, tire tread.
  • Heat: A/C health, coolant, engine temp.

Safety Tips & Essentials

  • Wear seatbelts — front and rear.
  • Never ignore warning lights.
  • Fire extinguisher + first-aid kit.
  • Check insurance, license, service expiry.
  • Use hands-free devices while driving.

Ownership Smart Tips

  • Read your owner’s manual.
  • Keep service records.
  • Know your drivetrain: FWD, AWD, 4x4.
  • Learn to jump-start or change a tire.
  • Use trusted mechanics.
